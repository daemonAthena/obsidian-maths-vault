A **binary number**, $b$, is an element from a [[Logical Operators#Variety Generation|Binary Algebra]] $\mathbb{B}$.

Some examples of binary numbers include:
- $0$, from $\mathbb{B}$
- $(1,0,0,1)$ from $\mathbb{B}^{4}$
- $(0,0)$ from $\mathbb{B}^{2}$


# Equivalence of binary numbers❓
Two binary numbers $b_{1}$ and $b_{2}$ are equal if they are equal after their trailing $0$'s are removed.

Example:
$(0,0,0,1,0) = (1,0)$
