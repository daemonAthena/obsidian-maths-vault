A **law** is a universally [[Quantifier|quantified]] equality of [[Operation#Terms|terms]].

For example, there is the law of commutativity:
	$\forall a,b$
	$a\cdot b = b \cdot a$
Notice there is the universal quantifier $\forall$
and an equality of terms.

Statements formed using the existential quantifier $\exists$ are not laws
for example there is the field axiom which states "All elements in $(\mathcal{S},\cdot)$ except $0$ must have an inverse." This is formally written as:
	 $\forall x, x\neq 0 \implies \exists \bar{x}$
There are many reasons why this is not a law:
- There is an inequality not an equality of terms
- it is an implication rather than an equality of terms
- it is not strictly universally quantified, there is an existential quantifier
